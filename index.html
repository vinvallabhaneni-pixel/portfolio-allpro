Background

How it started

I applied to AllProNY as a digital marketing intern. The agency was growing but had no structured digital marketing function. No consistent content, no analytics infrastructure, no paid media strategy. Growth at that stage came almost entirely through referrals from discharge planners, social workers, and case managers. Families searching for home care were a real audience the agency had no online presence with.

The first two months focused on understanding that dynamic. Both audiences needed different messages and different platforms. Referral partners respond to credibility and specificity. Families respond to clarity and reassurance. A single undifferentiated approach would not work for either.

After two months, the scope of the role changed. I was no longer assisting on tasks. I was setting direction. The promotion came after demonstrating measurable impact from the early SEO and content work, and with it came ownership of the full marketing lifecycle: strategy, execution, and infrastructure.

Work

The initiatives

01
SEO Campaign
AllProNY's first structured SEO effort. Keyword research scoped to NJ/NY home healthcare searches, on-page rewrites across core service pages, local citation work for NAP consistency, and content publishing tied to search intent. Views up 130% and unique visitors up 82% over 10 weeks compared to the prior period.
02
Paid Social Funnel on LinkedIn and Meta
Designed and launched paid campaigns targeting discharge planners, social workers, and case managers on LinkedIn, and family caregivers on Meta. Built audience segmentation, wrote ad copy, and structured budget allocation by funnel stage to keep costs low while reaching both audiences.
03
AllPro Elite Brand Launch
Developed the full digital marketing foundation for AllPro Elite, a new luxury private household staffing service under the AllProNY umbrella. Covered market positioning, a LinkedIn paid funnel targeting high-net-worth households, landing page architecture, a lead magnet strategy, and a stakeholder presentation to leadership.
04
Social Media Community Engagement
Built a consistent content and engagement cadence across LinkedIn and Meta. Wrote for two distinct voices: credibility-forward content for referral partners on LinkedIn, and empathy-led content for family caregivers on Facebook. Managed community responses and tracked engagement patterns to refine what resonated with each audience over time.
05
Referral Partner Outreach
Built outreach sequences for healthcare professionals, discharge planners, and community partners to support AllProNY's referral pipeline. Structured templates the team could use consistently while keeping the personal tone that relationship-based healthcare outreach requires.
06
Marketing Infrastructure
Designed the low-budget marketing stack AllProNY now runs on. GA4 with custom event tracking, a Node.js script connecting LinkedIn API data to GA4 via the Measurement Protocol, Python automation for recurring data tasks on Windows Task Scheduler, and a reporting framework that turns performance data into plain-language decisions for leadership.

Infrastructure

Built to stay lean

AllProNY at this stage could not carry a bloated martech stack. Every tool and process put in place was chosen because it delivered what the team needed at low or no additional cost. The goal was a setup that could scale with the team without requiring a dedicated person just to maintain it.

Analytics
GA4 + Measurement Protocol
Custom event tracking, conversion setup, and a Node.js script that pulls LinkedIn campaign data into GA4 as custom events. Unified paid and organic view without a paid attribution tool.
Automation
Python + Task Scheduler
Recurring data sync and content ideation workflows running automatically on Windows Task Scheduler. No third-party automation platform, no recurring SaaS cost.
SEO
GSC + Screaming Frog
Google Search Console for ongoing ranking and indexing visibility. Screaming Frog for crawl audits. Both at free or low-cost tiers covering everything needed at this stage.
Paid Media
LinkedIn + Meta, Budget-First
Campaigns built around a modest monthly budget. Audience segmentation and funnel stage allocation done manually to extract the most reach per dollar rather than relying on broad automated bidding.
